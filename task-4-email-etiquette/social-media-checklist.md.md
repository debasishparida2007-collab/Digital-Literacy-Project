**Responsible Social Media Use** 

(For College Students)

** Do’s**

•	Think before posting

Always consider how your post might be perceived by others, including professors, recruiters, or peers.

Example: Before posting a meme about college stress, check if it could be misunderstood as disrespectful. 

•	Maintain a professional and respectful tone

Use polite and appropriate language, especially on platforms related to academics or careers.

Example: When commenting on LinkedIn, write constructive and respectful replies instead of casual slang. 

•	Respect others’ opinions and boundaries

Engage in discussions calmly, even when opinions differ.

Example: Instead of saying “You’re wrong,” say “I see your point, but I have a different perspective.” 

•	Protect your personal information

Be mindful of what you share online and review your privacy settings regularly.

Example: Avoid posting your home address, college ID, or travel plans publicly. 

•	Verify information before sharing

Ensure that the content you share is accurate and from reliable sources.

Example: Before reposting news, check if it comes from a trusted website. 

•	Build a positive digital presence

Share achievements, projects, or learning experiences that reflect your growth.

Example: Posting about your coding project or internship experience. 

** Don’ts**

•	Don’t share offensive, abusive, or inappropriate content

Content that is disrespectful or controversial can harm your image.

Example: Avoid posting jokes that target specific communities or individuals. 

•	Don’t engage in online fights or trolling

Arguments on social media can escalate quickly and damage your reputation.

Example: Instead of replying angrily to criticism, ignore or respond calmly. 

•	Don’t overshare personal or sensitive details

Sharing too much can lead to privacy risks or misuse of information.

Example: Posting your phone number or live location publicly. 

•	Don’t post when emotional or angry

Emotional posts can lead to regret later. Take time before responding.

Example: Avoid posting a rant immediately after a bad exam or argument. 

•	Don’t copy or repost content without credit

Respect intellectual property and give proper credit to original creators.

Example: If you share someone’s artwork or notes, mention their name or source. 

•	Don’t ignore your digital footprint

What you post online stays for a long time and can be seen by future employers.

Example: Old inappropriate posts may affect internship or job opportunities.
